What if something goes wrong after you get home?

Published 17 July 2026· 7 min read

Nobody books treatment expecting a problem. But the honest question, the one worth asking any clinic, is what happens if there is one after you get home. A good answer is not "that will not happen". It is a clear plan for if it does. Here is ours, and what you should do at each stage.

First, how likely is it?

Most dental treatment settles without incident. Modern implants have long-term success rates well above ninety percent, and the majority of restorations do their job quietly for years. So the goal here is not to alarm you. It is to make sure that in the small number of cases where something needs attention, you are not left guessing. Knowing there is a plan is what lets you relax about the odds.

The kinds of things that can come up

After-treatment concerns tend to be minor and fixable when caught early. A crown that feels slightly high. A bit of ongoing sensitivity. A small adjustment to a bite. Occasionally something more involved. Almost all of it is manageable, and manageable is much easier when there is a route to help rather than a dead end.

The plan, not the promise

A good clinic does not say "nothing will go wrong". It tells you exactly what happens if something does.

What to do, step by step

1. Contact us first

Before anything else, get in touch. A quick message with a description and a photo often lets us tell you whether it is routine, whether it needs a local check, or whether an in-person review is the right move. Early contact turns small things into small things.

2. Get a local assessment if needed

If something needs eyes on it sooner than a trip allows, a local dentist can examine and document it. Through our UK partner network, that in-person review can happen at one of eight partner treatment centres across the UK, close to home.

3. Keep your paperwork

Your treatment plan and implant certificates matter here. They tell any dentist exactly what was done and what fits it. Keep them somewhere findable. Our article on implant certificates explains why.

4. Let us coordinate the fix

Depending on what it is, the answer might be a remote follow-up, a local adjustment, or arranging the right treatment. The point is that it is coordinated, not left to you to solve alone.

How to reduce the odds in the first place

The best way to handle problems is to make them less likely. Choose a clinic that plans properly with 3D imaging, uses documented brands, and gives you a written plan. Follow the aftercare instructions you are given in the first weeks, when healing matters most. Keep up your daily hygiene and your routine check-ups at home. And raise anything that feels off early, rather than waiting to see if it settles. Most serious issues start as small ones that were left too long. A patient who stays in touch and acts early rarely faces a big problem, which is the quiet reason good aftercare and good outcomes tend to go together.

Why this matters more than any guarantee wording

Guarantees are worth having, but a guarantee you can only claim by flying back is a weak safety net. A real aftercare pathway, contact, local review, coordination, is what makes the guarantee meaningful. It is the difference between a clinic that hopes for the best and one that has thought about the rest. You can read how our written guarantee and insurance work in the assurance section.
