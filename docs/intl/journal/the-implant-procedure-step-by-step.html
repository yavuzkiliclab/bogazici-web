The implant procedure step by step: what to expect

Published 17 July 2026· 6 min read

Fear of the unknown is what makes implant treatment sound daunting. Once you know what actually happens, and in what order, most of that worry fades. An implant is a planned, staged procedure, not a single dramatic event. This article walks through it step by step, so you arrive knowing exactly what to expect at each stage.

Stage 1: Examination and imaging

Everything starts with a proper assessment. A clinical examination and 3D tomography map your bone, so the dentist can see exactly what they are working with. At a modern clinic, impressions are taken with an intraoral scanner rather than uncomfortable trays. This stage is where your specific plan is built, and it is the foundation of a good result. Nothing is placed until the picture is complete.

Stage 2: Planning

Using the 3D imaging, the dentist plans the exact position, angle and size of each implant before touching anything. This digital planning is why a good implant is so precise: the hard decisions are made on the screen, in advance, not improvised in the chair. If any groundwork like grafting is needed, it is planned in here too.

The reassuring part

The difficult decisions happen on a screen before treatment. Placement itself is quick and precise.

Stage 3: Placement

The implant is placed into the jaw under local anaesthesia, with sedation available if you are anxious. Most people are surprised how straightforward this is. The surgery itself is usually a single, relatively quick session, and discomfort afterwards is typically mild and manageable with prescribed medication. This is the step people fear most and often find the least eventful.

Stage 4: Healing

Now the implant fuses with the bone, a process called osseointegration, which usually takes two to six months. You feel nothing of it. During this time you carry on normally, and a temporary tooth can often be fitted so you are never left with a visible gap. This wait is not wasted time. It is the foundation setting, and it is what makes the implant permanent. Our healing timeline article covers it in detail.

Stage 5: The final tooth

Once the implant has fused, the abutment is fitted and your permanent crown, bridge or arch is made and placed. At a clinic with its own laboratory, this is milled in-house for a precise fit. This is the moment the implant becomes a working tooth, and the treatment is complete. For patients travelling to Istanbul, stages three and five usually fall across two visits, planned around the healing period.

How anxious patients are looked after

If nerves are what is holding you back, you are far from alone, and a good clinic is set up for exactly this. Sedation is available for people who find dental treatment stressful, so the placement can be as calm as you need it to be. Beyond that, a lot of the anxiety comes from not knowing what is happening, which is why a clinic that talks you through each step as it goes makes such a difference. You can ask to understand what is being done and why, at any point. Many patients who dreaded the idea describe the actual placement afterwards as far less eventful than they had built it up to be. Being informed and being able to opt for sedation together take most of the fear out of it.

The whole journey in perspective

Laid out like this, an implant is a sequence of careful, planned steps, not a single ordeal. The placement is quick, the healing is invisible, and the result is a fixed tooth that can last decades. Knowing the order is often all it takes to turn dread into confidence. You can read the fuller clinical picture in our implant guide.
