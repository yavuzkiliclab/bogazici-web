NHS vs private vs Turkey: an honest look

Published 17 July 2026· 7 min read

For a UK patient, the real choice is often three-way: wait for the NHS, pay for private treatment at home, or travel to Turkey. Each has a genuine case, and the right answer depends on what you need and what you value. This article looks honestly at all three, so you can weigh them without a sales pitch pulling you one way.

The NHS option

NHS dentistry has a clear strength: cost. For treatment it covers, it is the most affordable option by far. The difficulty is access and scope. Finding an NHS dentist taking new patients has become hard in many areas, and the NHS does not routinely provide cosmetic work or the full range of implant treatment. For a check-up or basic treatment, the NHS is the sensible first port of call. For implants, full-arch work or a smile makeover, it is often simply not available.

The three-way trade

NHS: cheapest but limited and hard to access. Private UK: local but expensive. Turkey: big saving, short waits, aftercare now real.

Private UK dentistry

Private treatment at home offers the full range of work, high standards and the obvious benefit of being local, with your dentist down the road for any follow-up. The drawback is cost. Private UK dentistry is expensive, and for larger treatments like implants or a set of veneers, the figures are high. For patients who can afford it and value proximity above all, it is a strong choice. For many, though, the cost is the sticking point.

Treatment in Turkey

Turkey sits between the two on the things that matter: far cheaper than private UK care, with the full range of treatment the NHS often will not provide, and much shorter waits. The traditional drawback was aftercare and proximity. That is exactly the gap our UK partner network closes, with in-person aftercare at eight UK locations, as our aftercare article explains. With that in place, Turkey offers much of the private-UK range at a large saving, without giving up a route to help at home.

How to choose between them

Start with what you need. For routine or basic care, the NHS is the natural first choice if you can access it. For complex or cosmetic work, the NHS usually falls away, and the real choice is private UK versus Turkey. There, it comes down to cost against proximity. If being local matters most and cost is not the barrier, private UK. If the saving is significant and you want a real aftercare route without the private-UK price, Turkey. For larger treatments, the cost gap is often large enough to make Turkey compelling.

The honest summary

No single option wins for everyone. The NHS is cheapest but limited and often hard to access for this kind of work. Private UK is comprehensive and local but expensive. Turkey offers a large saving, short waits and, now, genuine UK aftercare, at the cost of a flight.
