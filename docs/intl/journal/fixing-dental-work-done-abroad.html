Fixing dental work done abroad: what to know

Published 17 July 2026· 7 min read

Not every story about treatment abroad is a happy one, and if you are reading this because something went wrong, or you are worried it might, this article is for you. Fixing dental work done abroad is possible, and understanding how it works removes a lot of the fear. This is a calm, practical guide to what to do, whether you are dealing with a problem now or simply want to know the safety net exists.

First, do not panic

Most problems with dental work, wherever it was done, are fixable, and many are minor. A crown that feels high, some sensitivity, a small adjustment, these are routine to put right. Even more significant issues can usually be resolved with a proper assessment and a plan. Panic leads to rushed decisions, which rarely help. The first step is always a calm, professional look at what is actually going on, not a hasty reaction.

The first move

Get a calm, professional assessment before doing anything. Most problems are more fixable than they feel.

Get a proper assessment

The starting point is an examination by a dentist who can see what was done and document it, with X-rays if needed. This establishes what is actually wrong, which is often less dramatic than it feels, and what the options are. Gather your paperwork first: your treatment plan, any implant certificates, and photos from before and after. This record helps whoever assesses you understand exactly what was done, which is why keeping it matters, as our certificate article explains.

Going back to the original clinic

A good clinic stands behind its work. If your treatment was done by a reputable clinic with a guarantee, the first route is usually back to them, and a clinic worth choosing makes this straightforward rather than difficult. This is exactly why aftercare and guarantees matter so much when you choose in the first place. The clinics that cause the horror stories are typically the ones that vanish afterwards, which is the opposite of what you want.

Where a UK route changes things

The traditional nightmare was needing to fly back for every problem. This is precisely the gap a real UK aftercare network closes. For our patients, an in-person assessment can be arranged at one of eight UK partner treatment centres, so a concern can be looked at close to home rather than only by flying back. Where an issue can be handled remotely, our photo follow-up deals with it. This is what turns a potential ordeal into something manageable.

If the original clinic is not an option

Sometimes going back is not possible, if the clinic was poor, unreachable or closed. In that case, a local dentist can assess and treat the problem, using your documentation to understand what was done. It may cost more than it should have, which is the real price of choosing badly in the first place, but the situation is almost always recoverable. This is the strongest argument for choosing carefully at the outset: a good clinic with real aftercare means you are very unlikely to end up here.

The lesson for choosing

The best way to deal with problems is to make them unlikely and, if they happen, easy to fix. That means choosing a licensed clinic with a genuine guarantee and a real aftercare route from the start. If you are choosing now, weight aftercare heavily, it is the thing you will be most grateful for if anything ever goes wrong. If you are already dealing with a problem, get a calm assessment and know that it is very likely fixable. Our safety guide covers choosing well.
