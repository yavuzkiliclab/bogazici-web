Bad breath: causes and what actually helps

Published 17 July 2026· 6 min read

Bad breath, known medically as halitosis, is common and often treatable, but it is surrounded by myths and quick fixes that do not address the real cause. If persistent bad breath bothers you, understanding what actually causes it, and what genuinely helps, is far more useful than reaching for another mint. This article gives you the honest picture.

Where bad breath usually comes from

The great majority of bad breath starts in the mouth. The most common source is bacteria, which gather on the teeth, gums and especially the tongue, breaking down food particles and releasing the compounds that smell. This is why oral hygiene is central to fresh breath, and why mints and mouthwash that only mask the smell provide brief cover rather than a real solution. Understanding that it is usually a bacterial issue in the mouth points to what actually works.

The real fix

Most bad breath is bacteria in the mouth, often on the tongue. Cleaning that source beats masking it.

The common causes

The usual culprits are: poor oral hygiene leaving bacteria and food particles, a coated tongue harbouring bacteria, gum disease, a dry mouth which lets bacteria flourish, and certain foods like garlic and onions. Smoking is another major contributor. Less commonly, persistent bad breath can relate to other health issues, which is why bad breath that does not respond to good oral care deserves mentioning to a dentist or doctor. But for most people, the cause is in the mouth and addressable.

What actually helps

Real, lasting fresh breath comes from tackling the source, not masking it. Thorough oral hygiene is the foundation: brushing twice a day, cleaning between the teeth daily, and importantly cleaning the tongue, where a lot of odour-causing bacteria sit. Staying hydrated helps by keeping the mouth from drying out. Regular dental check-ups catch gum disease or other issues early. These basics, done consistently, resolve most bad breath, which mints and mouthwash alone never will.

The role of gum disease

Persistent bad breath can be a sign of gum disease, where bacteria below the gum line cause both the odour and damage to the gums and bone. If your bad breath comes with bleeding gums, tenderness or looseness, get a dental assessment, since treating the gum disease addresses both the breath and the underlying health issue. This is one reason not to simply mask persistent bad breath, it can be telling you something that deserves acting on, as our gum health guide explains.

Quick daily habits for fresher breath

Alongside the basics, a few simple daily habits make a real difference and cost nothing. Clean your tongue every day, either with your toothbrush or a dedicated tongue scraper, since the back of the tongue is where a lot of odour-causing bacteria gather and where many people forget to clean. Drink water through the day, because a dry mouth lets bacteria flourish and is a common cause of breath that worsens between meals or first thing in the morning. If your mouth tends to run dry, sugar-free gum can help by stimulating saliva, which naturally cleans the mouth. Be aware that skipping meals or crash dieting can cause a distinctive breath odour too, so eating regularly helps. And if you smoke, know that it is one of the biggest contributors to persistent bad breath, on top of its other harms, so cutting down helps your breath as well as your health. None of these replaces good brushing, cleaning between the teeth and regular check-ups, but together they tilt the odds firmly toward fresh breath through the day.

When to see a dentist

If bad breath persists despite good oral hygiene, see a dentist. They can identify the cause, whether gum disease, a dental problem, or something needing referral, and treat it properly. Do not simply escalate the mints and mouthwash, which treat the symptom not the cause. For most people, good daily oral care including the tongue, staying hydrated, and regular check-ups keep breath fresh. Where it does not, a dentist finds and fixes the real reason.
