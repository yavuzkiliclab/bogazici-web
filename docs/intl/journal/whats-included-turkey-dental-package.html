What is included in a Turkey dental package?

Published 17 July 2026· 6 min read

Dental treatment abroad is often sold as a package, and the word can mean anything or nothing. A good package removes stress by handling the logistics around your treatment. A vague one hides gaps you only discover once you have arrived. This article explains what a proper package should include, and the questions that reveal what a quote actually covers.

What a package is meant to do

The point of a treatment package is simple: to let you focus on your treatment while someone else handles the practical parts of visiting a foreign city for dental work. Done well, it means you step off the plane and everything is arranged. Done badly, it means surprises and out-of-pocket extras at every turn. The difference is in the detail, so ask for the detail.

What a good package usually includes

  • The treatment itself, itemised, so you know exactly what work is covered.
  • Airport transfers, so you are met and moved between airport, hotel and clinic.
  • Accommodation, or help arranging it, for the nights your treatment requires.
  • Local transport between your hotel and the clinic for appointments.
  • A coordinator, a real person who manages your schedule and answers questions.
  • Aftercare arrangements, including what happens once you are back home.
The test question

"What is not included?" tells you more than "what is included?" A clear clinic answers both plainly.

What to check carefully

The gaps are where problems hide. Ask specifically: how many nights of accommodation are covered, and what happens if healing means you stay longer? Are all transfers included or just the first? Does the quote cover any extra procedures that might come up, like grafting? Is aftercare part of it, and what does that mean in practice? A clinic confident in its package answers these without hedging.

Why aftercare belongs in the package

Many packages end the moment you leave the clinic. The best ones treat aftercare as part of the deal, because your treatment is not finished when you fly home. For our patients, that includes the UK partner network for in-person review if it is ever needed. You can read how that works in our UK aftercare article.

Package or itemised: which is better?

Some patients prefer an all-in package for simplicity, others prefer everything itemised so they can see each cost. Neither is automatically better. What matters is transparency. A good all-in package clearly states what it covers and what it does not. A good itemised quote leaves nothing bundled out of sight. The danger is not the format, it is vagueness in either one. If you can see exactly what you are paying for, whichever way it is presented, you are in safe hands. Ask for whichever format helps you understand it best, and treat any reluctance to provide that clarity as the real warning sign.
