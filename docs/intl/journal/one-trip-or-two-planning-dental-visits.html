One trip or two? Planning your dental visits

Published 17 July 2026· 6 min read

When you travel for treatment, one of the biggest planning questions is whether you can get it all done in a single trip or need to come back. It affects your time off, your flights and your budget, so understanding it early helps. This article explains what decides one trip versus two, and how to plan for each.

What decides it

The deciding factor is simple: does your treatment need a healing period in the middle? Some treatments are completed start to finish in one visit. Others, above all implants, need months of healing between stages, which almost always means two trips. So the answer is set by your treatment, not by preference, and your dentist confirms it as part of your plan.

The deciding question

Does your treatment need months of healing in the middle? If yes, two trips. If no, usually one.

One-trip treatments

Veneers, crowns, and smile makeovers are usually one-trip treatments. Because they do not need the long fusing period an implant does, the whole process, preparation, laboratory work and fitting, can be completed within a single stay of several days to a week. For these, you plan one block of time, get everything done, and fly home with your finished result. Our how many days article covers the typical length.

Two-trip treatments

Implants and full-arch treatments usually need two trips because of osseointegration, the months-long fusing of the implant with the bone. The pattern is: first trip for examination and placement, a healing gap of a few months at home, then a second, shorter trip to fit the permanent teeth. Often a temporary tooth is fitted on the first visit so you are never left with a visible gap. Our healing timeline article explains the biology.

Can implants ever be one trip?

Sometimes elements can be combined, and in certain cases immediate temporary teeth are placed at the same time as the implants, so you leave the first trip with fixed-looking teeth. But the permanent restoration still waits for full healing. Be cautious of any offer to complete a full permanent implant treatment start to finish in a few days, as rushing the healing that makes an implant last is exactly the kind of corner-cutting that causes problems, as our what goes wrong article explains.

Planning for two trips

If your treatment needs two trips, plan for both from the start rather than being surprised by the second. Budget for two sets of flights and stays, as we cover in our budgeting article, and think about timing the healing gap around work or life. The upside is that each individual trip is usually short, and the gap between is spent living normally at home. Your coordinator helps you plan the timing of both visits so they fit your life.

The bottom line

One trip or two comes down to whether your treatment needs healing time in the middle. Veneers and crowns usually mean one trip; implants usually mean two. Neither is better or worse, they are just what the treatment requires. The important thing is to know which applies to you before you book, so your time off and budget are planned properly from the start. Your dentist and coordinator confirm your pattern as part of your treatment plan.
