Teeth whitening: your options explained

Published 17 July 2026· 6 min read

Teeth whitening is the most requested cosmetic dental treatment, and one of the simplest ways to improve a smile. But there is a lot of confusion about what works, what is safe, and what the various options really deliver. This article explains your whitening options honestly, so you can choose the right approach and avoid wasting money on things that do not work.

Why teeth discolour

Teeth darken and yellow over time for a few reasons: staining from coffee, tea, red wine and smoking, the natural ageing of teeth, and sometimes deeper discolouration from within the tooth. Understanding this matters, because different causes respond differently to whitening. Surface staining lifts well, while some deeper or age-related discolouration is more stubborn. A realistic view of why your teeth are discoloured helps set the right expectation for whitening.

The honest split

Professional whitening works well on staining and yellowing. It does not change crowns, veneers or fillings.

Professional whitening

Whitening carried out or supervised by a dentist is the most effective and reliable route. It uses stronger, controlled whitening agents than shop-bought products, applied safely, for a more noticeable and even result. It comes in two main forms: in-chair whitening done at the practice for a fast result, and custom take-home kits with professionally made trays for use at home. Both are supervised by a dentist, which keeps them safe and effective, and gives a better outcome than guessing with a generic product.

Shop-bought products

Over-the-counter whitening toothpastes, strips and kits are cheaper and widely available, and they can help with mild surface staining. But they are generally much weaker than professional whitening, so results are more limited, and generic trays fit poorly, which can cause uneven results or gum irritation. They are not useless for maintenance or minor staining, but for a real change, professional whitening delivers far more. Be wary too of unregulated online products making big claims.

What whitening cannot do

An important honest point: whitening only works on natural teeth. It does not change the colour of crowns, veneers or fillings, which keep their original shade. So if you have visible dental work, whitening can create a mismatch, and this needs planning with your dentist. And for teeth whose shape or alignment you also want to change, whitening alone will not do it, veneers may be the route, as our whitening vs veneers article explains. Whitening changes colour, nothing else.

Is whitening safe for your teeth?

A worry many people have is whether whitening damages the teeth, and it is a fair question given some of the alarming claims online. Done properly, professional whitening is safe. The whitening agents used by dentists are well established and, applied under supervision, do not harm the structure of the teeth. The most common side effect is temporary sensitivity, where teeth feel more reactive to hot and cold for a short time after whitening, and this settles on its own. A dentist can manage or reduce this, which is one advantage of the professional route over guessing with a strong shop-bought product. The real risks come from misuse: overusing high-strength products bought online without guidance, or using poorly fitting trays that let the gel irritate the gums. This is precisely why supervision matters. A dentist checks your teeth are suitable for whitening first, uses the right strength, protects your gums, and guides how often you whiten. Approached this way, whitening is one of the safest cosmetic treatments there is, which is part of why it is also the most popular.

Choosing the right approach

For most people wanting a brighter smile, professional whitening, whether in-chair or a supervised take-home kit, is the safest and most effective choice. Shop-bought products suit minor touch-ups and maintenance. If you have existing dental work or want to change more than colour, discuss it with your dentist first so the plan makes sense. And keep expectations realistic: whitening brightens your natural teeth, which for many is exactly the fresh, healthy improvement they wanted. Our aesthetics guide covers the wider cosmetic picture.
