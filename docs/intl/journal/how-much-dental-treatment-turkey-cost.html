How much does dental treatment in Turkey cost?

Published 17 July 2026· 6 min read

It is the question everyone wants answered first, and the one that is hardest to answer honestly in a single number: how much does dental treatment in Turkey cost? Anyone who quotes you a firm price before seeing your mouth is guessing. What this article can do is explain exactly what your price depends on, so you understand your quote when it comes and can spot one that does not add up.

Why there is no single price

Dental treatment is not an off-the-shelf product. The cost depends on what you actually need, and that varies enormously from person to person. Two people who both want "a new smile" can need very different treatment, one a handful of veneers, the other implants and bone work. So a real price comes after a proper assessment, not before. A trustworthy clinic gives you a clear, itemised quote once it understands your case, and holds to it.

The honest answer

A real price follows an assessment, never precedes it. Anyone quoting a firm figure sight unseen is guessing.

What your price actually depends on

The treatment you need

The single biggest factor. A few veneers, a single implant, a full arch, or a complex case with grafting all sit at very different points. Your quote reflects your mouth, not an average.

The number of teeth involved

More units, whether veneers, crowns or implants, means more materials and more chair time. This is usually the clearest driver of the total.

The materials chosen

Established implant brands and premium ceramics cost more than budget alternatives, and last accordingly. A quote should tell you which brands you are getting, so you can compare like with like. Our article on implant certificates explains why the brand matters.

Extra procedures

Some cases need groundwork first, such as extractions, bone grafting or a sinus lift. These add to the total but are sometimes essential for a lasting result.

How a good quote is structured

A quote you can trust is itemised. It lists each treatment, the number of units, the materials, and any extra procedures, with a clear total. Vague, lump-sum pricing that cannot be broken down is a warning sign. When you can see what each part costs, you can understand the price and compare it fairly. You can read what should be included in our article on dental packages.

Getting a meaningful estimate before you travel

You do not have to fly out to get a realistic idea of your cost. A good clinic can give you a well-informed estimate remotely, based on clear photos of your teeth and any recent X-rays you can send. This is not the final figure, that comes after a proper on-site examination, but it is close enough to plan around and far better than a number pulled from thin air. When you ask for an estimate, send the best information you can, and expect a range rather than a single figure until your case is fully assessed. An honest clinic will explain what could move the estimate up or down, so there are no surprises when the firm quote arrives.
