Turkey vs Hungary for dental work

Published 17 July 2026· 6 min read

Turkey and Hungary are two of Europe's biggest dental tourism destinations, and UK patients often weigh one against the other. Both have strong reputations, and both can be excellent choices. This is an honest comparison of the two, so you can see where each has an edge and decide which suits your treatment and your circumstances.

Two established destinations

Hungary, and its capital Budapest in particular, built its dental tourism reputation early, especially with patients from nearby European countries. Turkey has grown rapidly into one of the largest destinations in the world, with a very high volume of international patients and clinics geared for them. Both are serious, established options with plenty of experienced clinics. Neither is a fringe choice.

The short version

Hungary is closer for a quick trip. Turkey usually costs less and offers a fuller treatment-plus-travel package.

Cost

Both are cheaper than the UK. Between the two, Turkey is often the lower-cost option, particularly for larger treatments, thanks to lower overheads and the exchange rate. Hungary is still a strong saving against UK prices but tends to sit a little higher than Turkey. For a big treatment, the difference can be meaningful; for a smaller one, it may be marginal.

Travel and distance

Here Hungary has an advantage for many UK patients: Budapest is a shorter flight, which suits a quick trip or a treatment needing several short visits. Turkey is a longer flight, though still very accessible, and this is offset by clinics that fold travel, transfers and accommodation into a single organised package, which suits patients who want everything handled in one planned trip.

Quality and specialism

Both countries have excellent clinics and, as everywhere, weaker ones. Quality comes down to the specific clinic, its licensing, its dentists and its materials, not the country. Turkey's very high volume means many clinics are highly specialised in exactly the treatments international patients seek, with in-house labs and efficient workflows. Hungary has deep experience and a long track record. In both cases, the checks are the same: licensing, named dentists, documented brands, real aftercare, as in our safety guide.

Aftercare

Aftercare is the deciding factor for many, and worth asking about wherever you go. The key question is the same in both countries: what happens if you need help once you are home? A destination that offers a real aftercare route in your own country has a genuine advantage. Our UK partner network provides in-person aftercare at eight UK locations, which you can read about in our UK aftercare article.

Language and communication

A small but real factor is how easily you can communicate with the clinic, since a treatment plan you fully understand is safer and less stressful. Both Turkey and Hungary have clinics well used to English-speaking international patients, with coordinators and dentists who handle the whole process in English. This is not a meaningful point of difference between the two countries at the level of established international clinics, but confirm it for any specific clinic you consider, wherever it is. Ask whether you will have an English-speaking coordinator, whether your written plan and quote will be in English, and whether the dentist treating you speaks it directly. Clear communication throughout is one of the quiet markers of a clinic set up properly for international patients, and both destinations have plenty of clinics that do it well.

Which to choose

If a short flight for several quick visits matters most, Hungary's proximity is appealing. If you want the lowest cost for larger work and a fully organised trip with everything arranged, Turkey often has the edge. But the country is the second decision. The first is choosing a licensed, high-quality clinic with real aftercare, wherever it is. Get that right, and both Turkey and Hungary can deliver an excellent result.
