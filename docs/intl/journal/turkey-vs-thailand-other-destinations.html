Turkey vs Thailand and other destinations

Published 17 July 2026· 6 min read

Turkey is not the only country patients consider for dental treatment. Thailand, Mexico, Poland and others all have their advocates. For a UK patient, the choice usually comes down to a handful of practical factors. This article compares Turkey with the main alternatives honestly, so you can see where each makes sense.

The factors that actually decide it

Whatever the destination, four things really matter: cost, flight distance, the quality and specialism of clinics, and aftercare once you are home. Every comparison below comes back to these. The best destination for you is the one that balances them for your treatment and your circumstances, not simply the cheapest or the nearest.

The deciding four

Cost, distance, clinic quality, aftercare. Every destination is really a trade-off between these.

Turkey vs Thailand

Thailand is a well-regarded destination, especially for patients in Asia and Australia, and it combines treatment with a long-haul holiday. For UK patients, though, the flight is long and costly, which erodes the saving and makes several short visits impractical. Turkey is far closer to the UK, keeping travel cheaper and making two-visit treatments realistic. On cost, both are well below UK prices. For a UK patient specifically, Turkey's proximity usually makes it the more practical of the two.

Turkey vs Mexico

Mexico is a major destination for patients from the United States, who can often drive across the border, which is a huge advantage for them. For a UK patient, that advantage disappears: Mexico is a long, expensive flight. Turkey is much closer and better set up for European patients. Mexico makes great sense from the US. From the UK, Turkey is usually the more logical choice.

Turkey vs Poland

Poland, like Hungary, is a strong European option with a short flight and good clinics, appealing for quick trips. Turkey tends to be lower-cost for larger treatments and offers highly organised, all-in trips. The choice mirrors the Hungary comparison: Poland for proximity and quick visits, Turkey for cost on bigger work and a fully arranged experience. We cover the Hungary side in our Turkey vs Hungary article.

Why Turkey suits UK patients

For UK patients specifically, Turkey hits a practical sweet spot: a manageable flight, a large cost saving, a very high concentration of clinics specialised in international treatment, and increasingly, real aftercare back home. That last point matters. A destination on the other side of the world cannot offer in-person aftercare in the UK. Our UK partner network does, at eight UK locations, as our aftercare article explains.

Combining treatment with a holiday

One appeal of far-flung destinations like Thailand is turning treatment into a holiday, and that is a fair thing to want. But be realistic about how it works in practice. Dental treatment, especially anything involving surgery or multiple stages, is not always compatible with an active holiday: you may need rest, soft food and quiet days around procedures. A very long-haul trip built around a holiday can also clash with the calm recovery treatment needs. Turkey offers a middle path here. Istanbul is a genuinely rewarding city to spend time in between appointments, close enough to the UK that the trip is not exhausting, and clinics there are used to pacing treatment so patients can still enjoy the place. You get some of the holiday appeal without the long-haul toll or the pressure to sightsee when you should be resting.

The real conclusion

The best destination is not a fixed answer, it is the one that balances cost, distance, quality and aftercare for you. For a UK patient, Turkey usually strikes that balance well. But wherever you look, the same rule holds: choose a licensed, quality clinic with real aftercare over the cheapest option anywhere. The destination is secondary to the clinic.
