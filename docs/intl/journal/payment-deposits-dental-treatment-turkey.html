Payment, deposits and how it works

Published 17 July 2026· 6 min read

Money is where trust gets tested. How a clinic handles payment, deposits and the practicalities tells you a lot about how it operates. This article explains how payment usually works for treatment abroad, what is normal and what is not, so you can commit with confidence rather than confusion.

How payment usually works

For most treatment abroad, you pay for the treatment at the clinic, typically as you go through the stages rather than everything up front. A small deposit may be asked to secure your booking and dates, with the balance paid during treatment. What matters is that the amounts and timing are clear and agreed in advance, in writing, so there are no surprises about when money is due.

The principle

Clear, agreed, in writing. You should always know what you are paying, when, and for what.

Deposits: what is reasonable

A modest deposit to hold your appointment and coordinate your trip is normal and reasonable. It reflects the planning a clinic does to reserve time and arrange logistics. What is not reasonable is being pressured to pay large sums up front, before treatment and before you have seen anything. Be cautious of any clinic that wants most of the money before you arrive. A fair clinic ties payment to treatment stages, not to getting your money early.deposit terms to confirm

Paying in stages

For multi-stage treatment like implants, payment often follows the stages: a portion at placement, the balance when the final teeth are fitted. This aligns what you pay with what you receive, which is exactly how it should work. It also means you are never far ahead of the treatment you have actually had. Ask your clinic to explain the stage-by-stage schedule so you know what to expect at each visit.

Questions worth asking

  • How much is the deposit, and what does it secure?
  • When is the balance due, and is it tied to treatment stages?
  • What payment methods do you accept, and in which currency?
  • What happens to my deposit if I need to change my dates?

Clear answers to these are a good sign. Vague or evasive answers about money are a warning worth heeding.

A note on currency and card payments

It helps to understand how you will actually pay before you travel, so there are no awkward moments at the clinic. Ask which currencies the clinic accepts and whether card payments are possible, since relying on carrying large amounts of cash is neither safe nor necessary at a professional clinic. Check with your own bank about fees for card use or transfers abroad, as these small charges can add up. If you are paying in stages, confirm whether each stage can be paid the same way. Sorting this out in advance is a five-minute conversation that removes a surprisingly common source of last-minute stress, and a professional clinic will have clear, reassuring answers ready.

Our approach to payment

We keep payment clear and tied to your treatment, agreed in writing before you commit, with no pressure to pay large sums before you arrive. The exact schedule and terms are confirmed with your coordinator as part of your plan.
