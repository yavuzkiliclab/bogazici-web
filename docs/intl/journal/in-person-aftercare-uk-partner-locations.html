In-person aftercare at UK partner locations

Published 17 July 2026· 7 min read

When we say aftercare is available in the UK, it is fair to ask what that actually means. Vague reassurance is easy. A real network is not. This article explains exactly what our UK partner locations are, how being seen works, and why we are careful about the language we use to describe them.

What the UK partner locations are

Through our UK partner network, patients can be seen in person at one of eight partner treatment centres spread across the UK. These are established dental locations we have an arrangement with, so that a patient who has been treated in Istanbul and later has a concern can be assessed face to face without flying back. The point is proximity: help that is a drive away, not a flight away.

Precise language

We call them UK partner treatment centres, not "our clinics", because that is what they are. Honesty about this matters.

Why we choose our words carefully

You will notice we never call these "our UK clinics". That is deliberate. They are partner locations, independent centres we work with, not branches we own. Some clinics blur this line to sound bigger than they are. We would rather be accurate. When a patient understands exactly what is being offered, an independent UK partner network for in-person aftercare, they can trust the rest of what we say too. Overstating it would undermine the very reassurance it is meant to provide.

How being seen works

The process is straightforward. If you have a concern after returning home, you contact us first. We review it, and where a face-to-face look would help, we arrange for you to be seen at the partner location most convenient for you. For issues that can be handled remotely, we deal with them by photo and message instead, saving you a trip. You can read how that remote side works in our follow-up article.

Why this is rare, and why it matters

Most clinics treating international patients have no physical aftercare presence in the patient's home country at all. Their aftercare ends when you leave. A real UK network for in-person review is unusual precisely because it is harder to build than a promise. It is also the single thing that most directly answers the fear that stops people booking: being left alone with a problem, far from anyone who can help. You can see how this fits the whole safety picture in our safety guide.

What it means for your peace of mind

Knowing there is a real place you can go changes how the whole decision feels. Before treatment, it removes the biggest fear. After treatment, most patients never need it, but the knowing is what matters. It is like insurance in that sense: its value is not only in being used, it is in being there. Patients often tell us the UK network was the thing that finally made them comfortable booking, not because they expected problems, but because the alternative, being stranded with one, was the thing they could not accept. Removing that single worry is what the network is for.

The bottom line

Eight UK partner locations, described honestly for what they are, mean aftercare is not the weak point of treatment abroad for our patients. It is a strength. That is the difference between a clinic that hopes nothing goes wrong and one that has planned for the possibility that it might.
