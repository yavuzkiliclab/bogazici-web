How many days do you need in Istanbul for dental treatment?

Published 17 July 2026· 6 min read

One of the first practical questions once you are considering treatment is simple: how long do I actually need to be in Istanbul? The answer depends entirely on your treatment, and knowing the rough timings helps you plan work, time off and flights. This article breaks down how many days different treatments typically need, so you can plan realistically rather than guess.

Why the answer varies so much

There is no single number because dental treatments differ enormously in how they are done. Some are completed in a couple of sessions over a few days. Others, particularly implants, involve a healing period that means splitting treatment across two trips. So the honest starting point is that your stay is decided by your specific treatment plan, confirmed by your dentist. The ranges below are typical guides, not promises, and your coordinator gives you exact dates once your plan is set.

The key split

Veneers and crowns: often one trip of several days. Implants: usually two trips around a healing gap.

Veneers and smile makeovers

A veneer or crown treatment is often completed in a single trip, because it does not need the months of healing an implant does. Depending on the number of teeth, this typically means a stay of several days to a week, allowing for preparation, the laboratory work, and fitting. A clinic with its own laboratory can often work efficiently within this window. For many smile makeovers, one well-planned trip is enough.

Single implants

Implants are different because of osseointegration, the months-long fusing of the implant with the bone. This usually means two trips: a first for examination and placement, then a gap of a few months for healing at home, and a second, shorter trip to fit the permanent teeth. The first visit is often just a few days. Our healing timeline article explains why the gap is necessary.

Full-arch and complex cases

Full-arch treatments like All-on-4 also generally follow the two-trip pattern, with placement and a temporary set of teeth on the first visit, and the final fixed teeth after healing. Cases needing groundwork such as bone grafting may need an additional stage. These are the treatments where planning both trips from the start matters most, as we cover in our one trip or two article.

Booking time off work

A practical part of planning is knowing how much time to request off work, and it helps to ask your coordinator for a realistic figure before you book anything. For a single-trip veneer or crown treatment, that usually means booking the treatment days plus travel either side, and often a spare day for comfort. For a two-trip implant case, you are planning two shorter blocks rather than one long absence, which many people find easier to fit around work than one continuous stretch. Whatever your treatment, ask for the schedule in advance so you can request the right days rather than guessing, and build in a small margin in case lab work or healing needs a little longer. Knowing the real timing early means you can plan work, childcare and life around the trip instead of scrambling.

Planning around your stay

Whatever your treatment, build a little buffer into your plans. Allow a calm day or two around procedures rather than packing appointments into the tightest possible window, and leave room in case healing or lab work needs a little longer. Your coordinator plans the schedule with you and can tell you exactly how many days to book off and when.
